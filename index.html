Section 1 Recruitment Process

Step-by-Step Elimination

Four Common Recruitment Rounds

Most companies follow a structured elimination process. You must clear each stage to proceed to the next.

Aptitude Test

The "Fitness Test"

Tests numerical, logical, and verbal ability. It's about speed and accuracy.

Eliminates ~60%

Group Discussion

Teamwork & Comms

Evaluates communication, leadership, and ability to work in a team setting.

Eliminates ~20%

Technical Interview

Actual Skills

Deep dive into coding, core subjects, projects, and problem-solving skills.

Eliminates ~15%

HR Round

Culture Fit

Checks professionalism, confidence, attitude, and company alignment.

Final Selection

Each round eliminates candidates progressively. Preparation must be holistic.

Section 2 ATS Optimization

ATS-Friendly Resume Rules

Follow these 5 golden rules to ensure your resume beats the bot.

Simple & Clean Format

Stick to standard fonts (Arial, Calibri) and standard black text. Complexity confuses the parser.

No Tables or Fancy Designs

Avoid columns, tables, graphics, or headers/footers. ATS reads left-to-right, top-to-bottom.

Full of Keywords

Include exact keywords from the Job Description (e.g., "Python", "Project Management").

Easy to Read

Use clear, standard section headings (Experience, Education) so the bot knows where to look.

Think "Search Engine"

Write for the bot first. If your resume lacks the right terms, it remains invisible.

Section 4 Aptitude Preparation

Aptitude Test Basics

Quantitative

Number systems, algebra, geometry, data interpretation

Logical Reasoning

Patterns, sequences, puzzles, blood relations

Verbal Ability

Reading comprehension, grammar, vocabulary

Speed + Accuracy = Success

Most tests are timed. Practice under time pressure to build both speed and accuracy.

Key Aptitude Topics

  • Time & Work
  • Profit & Loss

Logical Reasoning

  • Series & Patterns
  • Coding-Decoding
  • Syllogisms
  • Puzzles

Week 3-4: Practice & Speed
